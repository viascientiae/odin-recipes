Apple Pie

Description

Apple Pie is the epitome of comfort and nostalgia, a timeless dessert that evokes memories of warm, cozy kitchens and family gatherings. This classic American pie features a flaky, buttery crust enveloping a luscious filling of sliced apples, sweetened with sugar and subtly spiced with cinnamon. As it bakes, the apples soften, releasing their natural juices and mingling with the spices, creating a fragrant and heartwarming aroma that fills the air.

Served fresh out of the oven, Apple Pie is best enjoyed with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream, allowing the contrast of warm, tender apples and the crisp, golden crust to dance on your taste buds. The balance of sweet and tart flavors in every bite is a testament to the perfection of this dessert.

Whether you're baking it for a special occasion or simply to savor the joy of homemade goodness, our Apple Pie recipe will guide you through the steps to create a pie that's a slice of pure comfort and tradition. Delight in the sweet simplicity of this beloved dessert and share it with loved ones, creating new memories to cherish.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Salt
  • Unsalted Butter
  • Cold Water
  • Apples
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Brown Sugar
  • Ground Cinnamon
  • Lemon Juice
  • Corn Starch
  • Eggs (for an egg wash)
  • Additional Granulated Sugar (for sprinkling on the crust)

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine 2 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt.
  2. Cut 1 cup of unsalted butter into small cubes and add it to the flour mixture.
  3. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add 6-8 tablespoons of cold water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together.
  5. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, wrap them in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  7. Peel, core, and slice 6-7 apples into thin, even slices.
  8. In a large bowl, combine the apple slices with 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, 1/4 cup of brown sugar, 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, and 2 tablespoons of cornstarch. Toss until the apples are coated.
  9. Roll out one of the chilled pie crusts on a floured surface to fit your pie dish.
  10. Place the rolled-out crust into the pie dish, pressing it gently against the sides.
  11. Pour the prepared apple filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly.
  12. Roll out the second chilled pie crust and place it over the apple filling.
  13. Trim any excess crust hanging over the edges and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  14. If desired, beat one egg and brush it over the top crust. Sprinkle with a little additional granulated sugar for a golden finish.
  15. Cut a few small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  16. Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  17. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least an hour before serving.
  18. Slice and enjoy your homemade Apple Pie, optionally serving it with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for an extra treat.
  19. Relish in the sweet, comforting aroma of freshly baked Apple Pie as you indulge in its perfect balance of sweet, tender apples and a flaky, golden crust. Whether it's a special occasion or a simple dessert to brighten any day, this classic pie embodies the joy of homemade goodness.
